About

Ph.D (Hydrodynamics), KTH, Royal Institue of Technology, Stockholm

I'm a researcher. In what you ask? Well, primarily in hydrodynamic stability but, also in computational methods, and eigenvalue algorithms, and aeroelasticity, and perturbation theory, and non-linear transition, and dynamical systems, and pattern formation, and statistical mechanics. I said I do research, I didn't say I publish!
Sometimes I do publish, if things look worthwhile. You'll find my publications list somewhere on the site. Mostly I work on things that catch my fancy. These days it's machine learning. Gotta figure out what all that hoo-haa is all about. I have to say, it's a lot more empirical than I had imagined. I'll get to unifying gravity and quantum mechanics eventually. Apparently we haven't managed to do that in a century. I guess there's no rush.

Onset of aeroelastic pitch-oscillations

Global stability analysis sheds light on the spontaneous onset of aeroelastic oscillations in a NACA0012 airfoil in transitional flow.

Linearized Fluid-Structure-Interaction (FSI)

An Eulerian formulation is developed for the linearized analysis of FSI problems with rigid-body-motion.

Pitch-oscillations of a Natural Laminar Flow (NLF) airfoil.

A quasi-linear model explains the non-linear behavior of aerodynamic forces for small amplitude oscillations in an NLF airfoil.
